Hello I'm

Yeshaswini Radhakrishnan

I am a passionate bioinformatics researcher blending computational genomics with molecular biology to unlock breakthroughs in personalized medicine. My expertise lies at the intersection of biology and computation, where I explore how algorithms and AI can be leveraged to understand complex biological systems.

Currently pursuing my MS in Bioinformatics at Northeastern University, I specialize in genomics, machine learning applications in biology, and computational drug discovery. My work revolves around analyzing large biological datasets, predicting disease-associated genetic variations, and developing computational models to accelerate therapeutic innovations.

Previously, I have led projects focusing on protein structure prediction, immune system interactions, and novel biomarker discovery for cancer research. My passion for bioinformatics extends beyond academia, as I actively contribute to open-source scientific computing projects and collaborate with researchers globally to advance personalized medicine.

Beyond research, I am an avid advocate for interdisciplinary learning, blending biotechnology, computational sciences, and clinical research to push the frontiers of biomedical advancements. My journey is fueled by curiosity, innovation, and an unwavering drive to make a tangible impact in the field of precision medicine.
